04 · Convertible Loan Note Structure

The Investment Structure for Eps — 1882 Energy Ventures

A Convertible Loan Note (CLN) is the right instrument here. It gives Eps downside protection (guaranteed return + interest) while preserving the upside of converting into Karnot equity at Series A — at a discount, because he took the early risk. The cash flow model above shows Karnot can repay this note in full from operations by Month 13. But Eps will want to convert instead.

📋 Note Terms

Principal$250,000
Interest rate (accruing annually)10% p.a.
Term24 months
Conversion triggerSeries A round
Conversion discount20% off Series A price
Security30× iHEAT fleet (~$180k book value)
Repayment optionAny time after Month 12

📊 Investor Return Scenarios

Scenario A — Repayment (Downside) $277,083
Karnot repays at Month 13: $250,000 principal + $27,083 interest (13 months × 10% p.a.). Simple 10.8% return. Fully covered by cash on hand.
Scenario B — Convert at Series A (Upside) Equity stake
At $396k/yr ARR × 10× SaaS/energy multiple = $3.96M valuation. $250k at 20% discount converts at $3.17M effective valuation = 7.9% equity stake in a company with $33k/month growing recurring revenue. The equity play is worth substantially more than the repayment.
Karnot's message to Eps:
"We will have the cash on hand to fully repay this note by Month 13 — but you will want to convert it to equity, because $33,000/month in recurring revenue is pushing our valuation rapidly."
Why this works for Eps / 1882 Energy Ventures: AboitizPower group needs to demonstrate active demand-side management programmes to the ERC and show innovation in their franchise areas. A 30-unit iHEAT VPP pilot in VECO territory gives 1882 a live DR showcase, a footprint in the Philippines energy tech ecosystem, and a convertible position in Karnot at pre-Series A pricing. The downside is a 10% guaranteed return backed by $180k in hardware assets. The upside is equity in a VPP operator with ₱396,000/yr ARR growing to ₱2M+.

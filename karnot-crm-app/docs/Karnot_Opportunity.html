The Opportunity: The "Quintuple Squeeze"

Heating and cooling account for approximately 50% of global final energy consumption. This massive demand is colliding with five converging global crises. Karnot's technology is uniquely engineered to address all these challenges simultaneously.

1. The War on Fossil Fuels

Governments across the UK, Canada, and EU are actively legislating bans on gas boilers to meet Net Zero commitments. This regulatory shift is creating a $300B annual replacement market as the world must rapidly electrify heat production systems.

2. The Chemical Cliff (PFAS Bans)

The current "solution" to fossil fuels (standard heat pumps) relies on refrigerants being banned globally as "Forever Chemicals" (PFAS). An estimated 95% of incumbent hardware will become obsolete by 2030, creating urgent demand for compliant alternatives like Karnot's natural refrigerant systems.

3. Food Insecurity

While 14% of global food production is lost due to inadequate cold chains, post-harvest losses in the Philippines can reach as high as 50%. Rising temperatures across ASEAN increasingly threaten livestock and crop yields, creating an urgent need for efficient, industrial-scale cooling solutions.

4. Economic Pressure (High Costs)

With Philippine electricity costs among the highest in Asia—dwarfing subsidised rates in Vietnam and Malaysia—our efficiency is not a luxury; it is an operational necessity for competitiveness.

5. Grid Instability

The rapid electrification of heating systems is putting unprecedented pressure on power grids worldwide. Utilities are increasingly demanding"Smart Thermal Assets"capable of intelligently shifting load away from peak hours to prevent system failures.

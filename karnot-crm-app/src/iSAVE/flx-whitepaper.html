02 — The FLX Solution

Passive Thermal Storage — Charged Free, Discharged on Demand

Core principle: During normal CO₂ compressor operation, high-pressure liquid refrigerant passes through a dedicated subcooling coil inside the FLX tank. This subcooling heat (otherwise wasted through the gas cooler) charges the PCM passively at up to 4.5 kWh/hr. When the evaporator coil needs defrosting, a valve opens the defrost coil circuit — stored heat flows to the frosted coil, melting up to 8.1 kg of ice in 8 minutes with zero impact on refrigeration duty.

Charge Mode

CO₂ high-pressure liquid passes subcooling coil inside FLX tank. PCM absorbs heat passively — no pumps, no additional compressor work. Charging rate: 4.5 kWh/hr. PCM melts progressively at ~24°C.

❄

Normal Defrost

iSAVE controller opens defrost coil valve. Stored PCM heat flows to frosted evaporator via defrost coil. 3 kW × 10 min = 5.4 kg ice melted. Refrigeration circuit stays active throughout.

⚡

Super-Boost Mode

3-way valve bypasses gas cooler — hot discharge gas (80–90°C) routed directly to FLX module. Achieves 4.5 kW defrost power, melting 8.1 kg ice in 8 minutes. For high frost-load conditions.

03 — Technical Architecture

Four Innovations. One Compact Module.

🧪
FLX Fluid 2.0 — Bio-Based PCM

Virgin coconut oil (VCO) base + 5% beeswax (raises melt point, prevents supercooling) + 3% PEG-1000 (nucleation agent, freeze-point depressant) + 4% graphene flakes (thermal conductivity enhancement). Phase change temperature: ~24°C. Latent heat: ~120 kJ/kg. Non-toxic, biodegradable, food-safe.

Melt point: 24°C · Latent heat: 120 kJ/kg · Density: 0.92 g/mL
🕸
Copper Metal Foam Matrix

Open-cell copper foam, 90% porosity, 10 PPI (pores per inch). Bulk thermal conductivity enhanced from 0.2 W/m·K (pure PCM) to 5–10 W/m·K. Provides structural support, prevents PCM stratification, and acts as a heat distribution network throughout the 14.93 L tank volume.

Porosity: 90% · Conductivity: 5–10 W/m·K · PPI: 10
🔊
Ultrasonic Agitation System

2× piezoelectric transducers, 100W each, operating at 20–40 kHz. Generates acoustic cavitation in liquid PCM, creating micro-jets that break up nucleation sites and prevent supercooling. Reduces PCM freeze time by 40% and melt time by 60% vs passive system. Total system power: 200W (activated only during phase transitions).

Frequency: 20–40 kHz · Power: 2×100W · Duration: transition only
⬛
Graphene Nanoflakes

4 wt% graphene flakes dispersed uniformly in PCM matrix. Intrinsic graphene thermal conductivity up to 5,300 W/m·K. In PCM composite: enhances bulk conductivity from ~7 W/m·K (foam only) to 13.5 W/m·K. Accelerates phase change by 40%, raises PCM utilisation from 67% (bare PCM) to 98%. Two-dimensional geometry maximises surface contact area.

Loading: 4 wt% · Final conductivity: 13.5 W/m·K · PCM util: 98%

05 — System Integration

Native to CO₂ — Retrofit or OEM

CO₂ as the working fluid is a prerequisite. FLX is designed specifically for transcritical CO₂ (R744) refrigeration and heat pump systems. The high operating pressures (80–150 bar high side) and unique subcritical/supercritical thermodynamic cycle create the subcooling opportunity that FLX exploits. FLX is fully compatible with R290 (propane) cascade systems where CO₂ is the low-temperature stage.

08 — Intellectual Property

Patent Position & IP Strategy

Karnot Provisional Patent Application — Filed 2025

Title: "FLX Thermal Defrost System Utilizing Bio-Based Phase Change Material, Graphene Nanoparticles, Ultrasonic Agitation, and Multi-Coil Thermal Exchange in CO₂ Heat Pump Applications"

CLAIM 1 — CORE SYSTEM

A thermal defrost system for CO₂ heat pump applications comprising: a PCM module with graphene nanoparticles for enhanced thermal conductivity; ultrasonic agitation transducers for phase transition control; multiple thermal exchange coils for subcooling heat capture and defrost discharge.

CLAIM 2 — PCM COMPOSITION

The system of Claim 1 wherein the phase change material is virgin coconut oil, formulated as FLX Fluid 2.0 (coconut oil base + 5% beeswax + 3% PEG-1000 + 4% graphene flakes), providing a phase change temperature of approximately 24°C.

CLAIM 3 — ULTRASONIC AGITATION

The system of Claim 1 wherein ultrasonic agitation operates specifically during freezing and melting phase transitions at 20–40 kHz to prevent supercooling, induce nucleation via acoustic cavitation, and accelerate heat transfer in the liquid PCM phase.

CLAIM 4 — TRIGGER MECHANISM

The system of Claim 1 wherein defrost initiation is triggered by fan motor differential pressure feedback, providing a direct physical measurement of frost accumulation on the evaporator coil without temperature sensors or timers.

CLAIM 5 — REFRIGERANT

The system of Claims 1–4 wherein the refrigerant is CO₂ (R744) operating in a transcritical cycle, utilising the subcritical–supercritical pressure differential to create the subcooling heat capture opportunity that charges the FLX PCM module.

ABSTRACT

This paper presents the iSAVE platform — an integrated edge-to-cloud energy asset management system developed by Karnot Energy Solutions INC for commercial and industrial deployment in the Philippines and Southeast Asia. The platform addresses four concurrent market failures: (1) the absence of affordable, standards-compliant demand response infrastructure for small-to-medium commercial sites; (2) the lack of verified, tamper-proof energy data for carbon credit issuance; (3) the unavailability of predictive maintenance for distributed heat pump and HVAC assets; and (4) the inability of distributed energy resources (DERs) to participate in grid services without expensive custom integration.

iSAVE resolves these failures through a novel two-device product family: the iSAVE Bridge — a PoE-powered Modbus-to-Ethernet field translator that installs at each asset — and the iSAVE Gateway — a Linux-based site controller that aggregates Bridge data, executes local control logic, and connects to the Karnot Control Centre cloud platform. Data integrity is guaranteed by a Rubidex permissioned blockchain layer. Environmental sensors are provided by Utterberry wireless IoT motes. Demand response is automated via OpenADR 2.0b, with fleet-level dispatch coordinated through the Octopus Kraken FLEX API and Philippine Meralco RDSP programme.

Section 1

Problem Statement

Why existing solutions fail the Philippines commercial energy market

The Philippines faces among the highest commercial electricity prices in Southeast Asia at ₱10–14/kWh — three to four times the cost in Malaysia or Vietnam. Despite this, commercial building operators have virtually no tools to actively manage their largest energy consumers: heat pumps, chillers, and HVAC systems that represent 60–70% of site energy consumption.

Demand Response (DR) programmes operated by Meralco (the RDSP scheme) and the IEMOP ancillary services market offer significant revenue potential — but require certified metering, verifiable load reduction data, and automated control systems that existing standalone building management systems (BMS) cannot provide at an accessible price point.

Carbon markets present a parallel opportunity. The Philippine Climate Change Commission's voluntary carbon framework and the Gold Standard international certification programme can monetise every kilowatt-hour of verified energy saving — but require immutable, auditable energy data that manual logging and traditional databases cannot guarantee.

Grid Problem: Philippine grid frequency instability and brownouts create demand for flexible, controllable loads — but no affordable aggregation infrastructure exists for commercial sites below 1MW.
Data Problem: Utility DR payments require proof of load shed. Carbon registries require immutable energy data. Traditional databases can be edited — making them unacceptable for either purpose.
Hardware Problem: Industrial energy management gateways cost $800–3,000 per site and require specialist commissioning. This economics prevents deployment at the scale needed to create a viable demand response fleet.
Maintenance Problem: Heat pump failures are costly and unpredictable. No affordable remote monitoring solution exists that can provide the sensor granularity needed for predictive maintenance at field scale.

Running a single Cat6 cable (which is standard network cable, cheap, and understood by every electrician) is vastly simpler. Put the "translation" intelligence at the asset — not in the central cabinet. The iSAVE Bridge makes every device a standard Ethernet/MQTT device in under 10 minutes.

Section 3

Full Platform Integration

How Bridge + Gateway + Cloud + Blockchain + DR Relay all work together from a single sensor reading to a verified carbon credit

🔬 Utterberry Sensor Layer

Utterberry wireless motes (temperature, vibration, humidity, pressure) self-organise into a Sub-GHz mesh. The Utterberry Base Station (LAN-connected to the iSAVE Gateway) aggregates all mote data. This provides the environmental context that Modbus data alone cannot — compressor vibration signatures for bearing wear detection, evaporator surface temperature for defrost optimisation, ambient humidity for mould risk scoring. Complementary to iSAVE Bridges: Bridges read the machine data (power, setpoints, fault codes); Utterberry reads the physical state of the machine.

⚡ GUDE DR Relay Layer

GUDE Expert Net Control units (2302-1: 4-channel relay; 2314-1: 3-channel relay + energy metering) connect to the Gateway via LAN. When the Gateway receives a DR command (from Karnot Cloud via OpenADR 2.0b), it sends HTTP commands to the GUDE units to switch relevant loads. The 2314-1 model measures actual kWh per output channel — providing utility-grade proof of load reduction for Meralco RDSP payment claims. GUDE response time: <100ms. All switching events logged to Rubidex blockchain for immutable DR audit trail.

🔗 Rubidex Blockchain Layer

Every 15 minutes, the iSAVE Gateway bundles: energy meter readings (kWh), Bridge telemetry snapshots, active DR event status, Utterberry sensor averages. This bundle is SHA-256 hashed, signed with the Gateway's ATECC608B hardware key, and submitted to Rubidex's permissioned blockchain via their API. The hash is immutably committed — if anyone alters the data later, the hash won't match. Utilities (Meralco) and carbon registries (Gold Standard) can independently verify any historical reading via Rubidex's audit API. This turns raw IoT data into legally defensible evidence.

🌐 Karnot Cloud + Kraken/RDSP

The Karnot Control Centre (EMQX + TimescaleDB + FastAPI + React) provides the fleet management layer. OpenADR 2.0b VTN server issues DR signals to all Gateways simultaneously. AI forecasting (Python Prophet + scikit-learn) predicts site demand and solar generation 24 hours ahead — enabling optimal pre-conditioning before DR events. The Kraken FLEX API integration means Karnot registers as a FLEX asset aggregator: the fleet's available DR capacity is published to Kraken every 30 minutes; Kraken or Meralco RDSP dispatches events; Karnot translates these into per-site OpenADR commands. DR revenue flows: Kraken/RDSP → Karnot → site owner via Rubidex smart contract.

System and Method for Blockchain-Verified Demand Response and Carbon Credit Generation in Distributed Energy Asset Networks Using PoE-Powered Edge Translation Devices

Inventors: Stuart Cox, Karnot Energy Solutions INC  ·  Field: Energy Management Systems, IoT, Blockchain, Demand Response  ·  Filing Jurisdiction: Philippines (IPOPHL) + PCT international
Patent Section 1

Background of the Invention

Distributed energy resources (DERs) including heat pumps, solar photovoltaic systems, thermal energy storage systems, and battery energy storage systems (BESS) present significant opportunity for demand response (DR) programme participation and carbon credit generation. However, existing systems suffer from three critical limitations that this invention addresses.

First, integration of DERs with demand response infrastructure requires either expensive custom integration (costing $800–3,000 per site) or proprietary communication protocols that create vendor lock-in. No affordable, standards-compliant field device exists that can translate between the RS485/Modbus RTU protocols used by most heat pumps and the Ethernet/TCP/IP infrastructure required by cloud-based DR systems.

Second, carbon credit issuance under established methodologies (Gold Standard, Verra VCS) requires tamper-proof, auditable energy measurement data. Traditional database systems do not provide cryptographic proof of data integrity, requiring expensive third-party auditing that makes small-scale carbon credit programmes economically unviable.

Third, predictive maintenance for distributed heat pump fleets lacks affordable, wireless sensor solutions with sufficient granularity to detect early-stage mechanical failures (compressor bearing wear, refrigerant degradation, condenser fouling) before they result in costly breakdowns.

Patent Section 2

Summary of the Invention

The present invention provides a system and method comprising three novel components that, in combination, constitute a non-obvious and inventive solution to the above limitations:

Novel Element A

The iSAVE Bridge Device: A Power-over-Ethernet (PoE) powered field translation device comprising a hardware secure element, RS485/Modbus RTU interface, and Ethernet/MQTT output — providing cryptographically-signed energy data from distributed assets.

Novel Element B

The Verified DR Method: A method for blockchain-verified demand response event recording using hardware-signed data bundles committed to a permissioned blockchain, providing utility-grade proof of load reduction without third-party auditing.

Novel Element C

The Carbon Pipeline Method: A method for autonomous carbon credit generation using on-device baseline comparison, hardware-signed avoided emissions calculation, and blockchain-committed Measurement, Reporting & Verification (MRV) data for direct registry submission.

Independent Claim 4 — Method Claim

Method for Blockchain-Verified Demand Response

A computer-implemented method for verifiable demand response event recording, the method comprising the steps of:

  1. receiving a demand response event signal at a site controller from an automated demand response (OpenADR 2.0b) virtual top node;
  2. issuing, by the site controller, one or more control commands to energy assets via Modbus TCP or SG-Ready relay contacts to curtail or modify their energy consumption;
  3. measuring, via hardware-secured field devices, actual energy consumption during and after the demand response event period;
  4. calculating the load reduction achieved as the difference between measured consumption and a pre-established baseline consumption model;
  5. constructing an event record comprising: event identifier, start timestamp, end timestamp, commanded load reduction target, achieved load reduction in kWh, per-asset measurement data, and a hardware cryptographic signature;
  6. submitting said event record to a permissioned blockchain system for immutable storage;
  7. providing a blockchain transaction identifier to the demand response programme operator as auditable proof of load reduction.
Independent Claim 5 — Method Claim

Method for Autonomous Carbon Credit MRV

A method for automated Measurement, Reporting and Verification (MRV) of greenhouse gas emission reductions for carbon credit issuance, the method comprising:

  1. establishing, at initial system installation, a baseline energy consumption model for a monitored site, said baseline being committed to a permissioned blockchain with a hardware-signed device certificate;
  2. continuously measuring actual energy consumption of said site using hardware-secured metering devices at a defined interval;
  3. calculating, for each measurement interval, the avoided energy consumption as the difference between the baseline model and the actual measurement, adjusted for relevant variables including ambient temperature and occupancy;
  4. applying a grid emission factor specific to the site's electricity supply region to convert avoided energy consumption to avoided greenhouse gas emissions in CO₂-equivalent tonnes;
  5. committing signed avoided emissions records to a permissioned blockchain at each measurement interval, thereby creating a tamper-proof emissions reduction audit trail;
  6. aggregating committed records over a reporting period and submitting aggregated avoided emissions data and corresponding blockchain transaction identifiers to a carbon credit registry for automated credit issuance.

Patent Section 4

Abstract (for Patent Filing)

An energy asset management system and associated methods are disclosed. The system comprises PoE-powered field translation devices (iSAVE Bridge) that convert RS485/Modbus RTU communications from energy assets (heat pumps, solar inverters, battery storage, thermal storage) into MQTT messages over standard Ethernet, wherein each message is cryptographically signed using a hardware secure element containing a unique device private key. A site gateway controller aggregates signed data from multiple field devices, executes local demand response and energy optimisation logic, and submits periodic data bundles to a permissioned blockchain for immutable storage. A cloud platform provides fleet management, OpenADR 2.0b demand response coordination, and automated carbon credit MRV via the blockchain audit trail. The system enables verifiable demand response participation and autonomous carbon credit generation without third-party auditing, reducing the cost and complexity of DR programme participation and carbon market access for small-to-medium commercial energy asset operators.

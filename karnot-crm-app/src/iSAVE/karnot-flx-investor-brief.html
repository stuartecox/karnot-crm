The Problem

Defrost Is Broken — In Three Different Ways

Every cold storage facility in the world runs defrost cycles. Most lose 20–30% of their total electricity to it. And the current "best practices" carry either huge energy cost or serious safety risk.

⚡

Energy Waste

  • 20–30% of all cold store energy is wasted on defrost
  • Electric heaters strip heat you just paid to remove
  • UK 500 kW cold store: £60k–£90k/yr defrost electricity
  • Philippines (₱14/kWh): ₱420k–₱630k/yr per store
  • Global market: ~$2.8B/yr in defrost electricity waste
  • Danfoss 2017: "Electric defrost is very expensive operationally"
💥

Safety Risk (Hot Gas)

  • Hot gas defrost = current "best practice"
  • Liquid hammer: rapid pressure transients cause failures
  • Condensation-induced shock: pipe and valve damage
  • Danfoss 2017 identified as primary safety risk in NH₃
  • Repair costs: £500k–£2M per major incident
  • FLX eliminates this category entirely — no hot gas
🌡️

HFC Lock-In

  • Electric defrost designed around HFC refrigerants
  • R404A: GWP 3,922 — Kigali illegal by 2028
  • CO₂ transcritical is the future — but needs new defrost
  • CO₂ Cp spike near 35°C: unique subcooling heat window
  • FLX exploits this CO₂ property exclusively
  • FLX only works with CO₂ systems — this is the moat
Danfoss Industry Baseline (Vestergaard, 2017): Independent study on ammonia hot gas defrost systems confirms: electric defrost "very expensive operationally." Field test on 41 kW evaporator: 12.6 kWh saved per cycle using optimised hot gas vs electric. FLX targets near-zero defrost energy cost — exceeding even the best hot gas methods, without their safety risks.
The Technology

Passive. Thermodynamically Verified. Patent-Defended.

FLX uses waste heat from CO₂ gas cooler subcooling to charge a Phase Change Material thermal battery. When defrost is needed, the PCM discharges — melting frost without any electric heater or hot gas event.

1
CO₂ Subcooling Coil — Passive Charging

Supercritical CO₂ (~37°C, 80–150 bar) after gas cooler passes through FLX heat exchanger. Near pseudocritical temperature, CO₂ Cp spikes — more heat per degree than any HFC refrigerant. Deposits 4.5 kWh/hr into PCM. Zero extra energy. First-principles verified: 0.17 kg/s × 2.0 kJ/kg·K × 13°C = 4.4 kW ≈ claimed. ✅

2
FLX Fluid 2.0 — PCM Matrix

Virgin coconut oil base: 24°C melt point, 120 kJ/kg latent heat. Enhanced with 5% beeswax (melt stabiliser), 3% PEG-1000 (crystal modifier), 4% graphene nanoflakes (conductivity). Nickel-plated copper foam matrix: prevents lauric acid corrosion. Full stack: 0.5 → 13.5 W/m·K. Melt point & latent heat: literature-confirmed ✅

3
Fan ΔP Defrost Trigger — Patent Claim 4

As frost accumulates on evaporator fins, airflow resistance increases. Fan differential pressure rises. FLX triggers defrost when ΔP exceeds threshold — not a timer, not a temperature sensor. No prior art found. Peer review: novel and physically sound ✅

4
Ultrasonic Agitation — Supercooling Prevention

2 × 100W piezoelectric transducers at 20–40 kHz. Activated during phase transitions only. Prevents supercooling (delayed solidification). Enhances solid-liquid heat transfer. Valid mechanism — scientifically well-established ✅. Cavitation erosion test required (1,000 cycles).

5
DR-Deferred Defrost — Patent Claim 2

When iSAVE receives Meralco/VECO DR event, FLX holds defrost for up to 4 hours even after frost threshold reached. No competitor offers this. Cold store participates in demand response without product risk. Peer review: genuine novelty, no prior art ✅

6
Super-Boost Mode — Energy Balance Resolver

3-way valve routes hot discharge gas (80–90°C) through FLX when PCM charge is low. 4.5 kW × 8 min = 8.1 kg ice melt. Resolves energy balance gap for heavy frost conditions. Maintains defrost performance at full range of operating conditions.
